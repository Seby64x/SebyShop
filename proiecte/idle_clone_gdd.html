Core Engine & Multi-Character System

Family account, AFK calculation, and slot progression

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Account System

All characters on an account share a unified Family object. The three pillars of account-wide data are:

🏦
Shared Bank
A communal storage accessible by all characters. Items deposited are visible to every slot. Coins, materials, and equipment are transferable with zero cost. Capacity scales with the Bank Expansion stamp upgrade.
🏆
Account Achievements
Milestones tracked per-account (not per-character). Earning an achievement unlocks a permanent Account Bonus — typically a flat or % buff applied to all characters simultaneously.
📊
Account Bonuses
A stack of passive multipliers derived from achievements, the Codex, and W4 Laboratory nodes. Applied before individual character calculations. Examples: +5% all EXP, +10% AFK Gains, +2 base inventory slots.

⏱ AFK Gains Formula
Gains = Time_Offline × Kills_per_Hour × Drop_Rate × AFK_Efficiency

// Where:
Time_Offline = seconds AFK (capped at 10 hours base, extended by stamps)
Kills_per_Hour = floor(char_damage / monster_HP) × 3600
Drop_Rate = item_base_rate × (1 + DR_bonus / 100)
AFK_Efficiency = (AFK_talent_pts × 0.5) + stamp_bonus + lab_node_bonus

Drop Rate bonuses stack additively from Stamps, Alchemy bubbles, Cards, and the W4 Laboratory. AFK Efficiency caps at 500% but individual sources are uncapped.

EXP Formula
EXP_gained = Kills_per_Hour × monster_base_xp × EXP_mult × Time_Offline
// EXP_mult = (1 + stamp%) × (1 + bubble%) × (1 + alchemy_vial%)
W1

World 1 — The Foundation

Forest / Mining biome · Smithing · Stamps · Statues

⚒ Smithing System

Smithing is the core crafting engine. Characters use Anvils to produce gear and intermediate materials. Two operational modes exist:

🔨
Crafting Tab
Player selects a recipe and manually crafts items. Requires materials in inventory. Produces finished equipment (weapons, armor, tools). Each craft consumes Anvil Production Points.
🏭
Production Tab (Passive)
Set an Anvil to passively generate intermediate materials: Thread, Nails, Leather Strips. Generates at a rate of PP/hr × Efficiency_mult. Collected on login, no cap during AFK beyond the 10hr window.
Anvil Upgrade Path
Anvil I (Wood)+0 PP
Anvil II (Bronze)+50 PP/hr
Anvil III (Iron)+200 PP/hr
Anvil IV (Gold)+1000 PP/hr
📮 Stamps Collection

Stamps are one-time collectible items turned in at the Stamp Lady NPC. Each stamp occupies a permanent slot and provides a flat or % bonus that is upgraded using coins + materials.

Stamp Categories
Combat Skills Misc Fishing Alchemy Lab
Upgrade Cost Formula
Cost(lv) = base_coin × (1.35 ^ lv) × (1 + material_req(lv) × mat_cost)
// Exponential scaling — early levels cheap, later levels extreme

Example stamp bonuses: Sword Stamp +1% damage/lv, HP Stamp +20 HP/lv, Speed Stamp +1% skill speed/lv

🗿 Stat Statues

Stone Statues are dropped by monsters or mined. Turning them in at the Sculptor NPC grants a permanent buff. Each statue type corresponds to one stat. Statues stack — handing in 100 Strength Statues gives more than 1×100.

World 2 — Complexity & Scaling

Desert biome · Alchemy · Post Office · Obols

🧪 Alchemy System — The Core Multiplier Engine

Alchemy is the single most impactful progression system in the game. It has two sub-systems: Bubbles and Vials.

Bubbles — 4 Cauldrons
🔴 STR
Warriors · Physical bonuses · DMG multipliers
🟢 AGI
Archers · Speed · Critical chance
🔵 WIS
Mages · Skill XP · Talent points
🟡 LUK
Journeyman · Drop Rate · Gold find

Players spend liquids to "brew" new bubbles (gacha unlock from a pool of ~25 per cauldron). Each bubble has its own upgrade cost scaling using the relevant material + liquid type.

Bubble Upgrade Formula
effect(lv) = base_value × (1 + (lv × power_coefficient))
cost(lv) = mat_base × (1.37 ^ lv)
// Most bubbles provide multiplicative bonuses to account-wide stats
Vials

Players deposit specific materials into vial slots. Each material type unlocks one bonus (e.g., Copper Ore → +Smithing Speed). Vials are upgraded by depositing more of the same material. Effect scales linearly with vial level, capped at level 13.

Copper Ore → Smithing Speed Jungle Logs → AFK Gains Sand Giants Gut → Drop Rate Mushroom Cap → Skill EXP
📦 Post Office System

A daily delivery system. Each day, the Post Office generates a list of resource requests (Deliver X amount of Y material). Completing deliveries earns Box Points per box type.

📬
Box Upgrade Trees
Each box type has an upgrade tree (3 rows × 5 levels). Points spent unlock stat increases: DMG%, DEF, Carry Cap, AFK Gains, etc. Points are per-character — not shared.
📅
Daily Reset & Streak
Deliveries reset every 24h. Completing all daily deliveries for 7 consecutive days grants a Streak Bonus of 2× Box Points for that day.
⭕ Obol Grid System

Obols are circular/square/sparkle-shaped items that slot into a character's Obol grid (a 4×4 matrix). Matching adjacent Obols of the same type grants bonus multipliers via a family bonus system.

W3

World 3 — Automation & Construction

Ice biome · Construction · Library · Worship · Trapping

🏗 Construction System

Construction is the automation backbone of the late game. Players build structures on a board, each consuming Build Points generated passively by Construction-leveled characters.

Building Types
⛩
Shrines
Placed on the world map, they buff all characters fighting in that specific map zone. Example: EXP Shrine → +% combat XP in placed zone. Shrine strength scales with Construction level and time active on the map.
🧂
Salt Refinery
Converts raw materials into Salt types (Red, Orange, Blue, Purple, Nullo). Salt is the primary fuel for W3 and W4 crafting. Refinery upgrades increase output rate and unlock higher-tier salts.
🖨
3D Printer
A character "samples" a resource (e.g., Copper Ore at 2,000/hr collection rate). The Printer passively generates that resource without the character being present. Sample slots scale with Printer upgrade level (base 2 slots → max 6).
3D Printer Formula
Print_rate = sampled_rate × (printer_efficiency / 100) × salt_cost_mult
// sampled_rate = actual collection rate at time of sampling
// printer_efficiency upgrades via Construction board build
📚 Library System

The Library allows players to expand the talent cap for individual skills beyond their natural ceiling. By spending Book Stacks (obtained via Book Bundles drop or purchase), players push a talent from its base cap to an extended cap.

Extended_cap = base_cap + floor(book_stacks_spent / threshold)
// Example: Talent "Sharpened Axe" base cap = 100, extended cap up to 145
// Each +5 cap costs an increasing number of Book Stacks
🗼 Worship Skill

Worship is a semi-active skill. Players plant a Totem on a map and engage in a tower-defense minigame where enemies approach the totem in waves. Surviving waves charges the Totem's Charge bar. Full Charge is redeemed at the Worship altar for bonus Souls (currency for W3 upgrades).

🦝 Trapping Skill

Characters set physical traps on maps and return after a time period to collect trapped critters. Each critter type yields a unique material used in W3/W4 crafting recipes. Trap efficiency scales with Trapping level and trap quality crafted via Smithing.

W4

World 4 — The Meta-Game

Space biome · Cooking · Pet Breeding · Laboratory

🍳 Cooking System

Cooking uses Kitchens (purchased with Spice, earned via Breeding) to discover recipes and produce Plates. Plates grant global account-wide bonuses and are the primary scaling mechanism of W4.

🔬
Recipe Discovery
Players spend Kitchen fire speed to attempt recipe combinations. Discovery is RNG-gated but the recipe pool is pre-determined. A Spice Contribution mechanic speeds up fire without burning kitchens.
🍽
Plate Bonuses
Each discovered recipe, when cooked to completion, permanently activates a Plate Bonus: +% Damage, +% Skill EXP, +% AFK Gains, etc. Plates are tiered (Silver → Gold → Diamond). Higher tiers give multiplicative upgrades to the same bonus.
plate_bonus(tier) = base_bonus × (1.25 ^ tier_level)
// tier 0=Silver, 1=Gold, 2=Diamond, 3=Onyx (endgame)
🐾 Pet Breeding System

A gacha-style system where captured pets (found via W4 maps) are bred in the Fenceyard. Breeding produces new pets with inherited or mutated genes. Pets serve two purposes: Arena combat (for Spice drops) and passive Spice income.

🧬
Gene System
Each pet has 1–3 Genes determining combat ability: Rattler (AoE burst), Refiller (ability cooldown reset), Defender (damage reduction). Genes mutate at a low % per breeding attempt.
🌶
Spice Economy
Arena battles drop Spice types (Red, Green, Yellow, Purple, Void). Spice fuels Kitchen fire and is used to purchase additional Kitchens. Spice income rate is the chief bottleneck for Cooking progression.
🔬 The Laboratory

The Laboratory is the account-wide force-multiplier. Characters must be placed "inside a tube" (effectively set to Lab AFK instead of combat or skilling). While in the Lab, a character powers Nodes and Jewels.

🔵
Nodes
Nodes connect along a circuit path. Each powered Node doubles or triples a specific account bonus. Examples: Node A → ×2 Alchemy bubble effect, Node B → ×1.5 Cooking plate bonus. A Node activates when its "power requirement" is met by connected characters.
💎
Jewels
Special nodes requiring a crafted Jewel item to activate. Jewels provide the strongest single bonuses in the game. Jewel crafting requires W3 Salts + rare W4 drops. Once socketed, a Jewel is permanent and cannot be removed.
lab_power(char) = base_power + (lab_level × 10) + (chip_bonus)
node_active = (total_connected_power ≥ node.requirement)
// Characters with higher Lab level contribute more power to the circuit

📋 Talent Tab Structure
①
Tab 1 — Base Class Talents
Shared across all characters of the same base class. Contains foundational combat and survival talents. Max level 100 base, extendable via Library.
②
Tab 2 — Sub-Class Talents
Unlocked after W2 class promotion. Contains role-defining skills unique to the sub-class. Affects playstyle: Barbarian gains AFK-focused talents, Squire gains Construction synergies.
③
Tab 3 — Elite / Skill-Specific
Unlocked after W4 Elite promotion. Contains the most powerful multipliers in the game. Also includes skill-specific talent pages (Mining, Fishing, etc.) unlocked by skill level thresholds.

🎨 Visual Style Guide
🖼
Perspective: 2.5D Side-Scrolling
Characters and monsters rendered as pixel sprites on a layered parallax background. Foreground layer (ground/obstacles), mid layer (characters/enemies), far layer (sky/backdrop). Each world has a distinct color palette.
🎮
Pixel Art Aesthetic
16×16 to 32×32 character sprites. 64×64 boss sprites. Chunky, vibrant outlines. Animations: idle (4f), walk (6f), attack (8f), death (10f). UI elements follow pixel grid with 2x scaling on HD displays.
🌈
World Color Palettes
W1 Forest: greens + warm browns. W2 Desert: amber + sandy tans + orange sunset. W3 Ice: cool blues + white + cyan sparkles. W4 Space: deep purples + starfield blacks + neon accent pops.
